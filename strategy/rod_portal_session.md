## 3. Answers to (a)–(e)

### (a) Conveyance chain 2002 → present for the block

**[fact]** BS&A's Comments field (assessor's own working notes, quoted in full in §1) lists 29 recorded-instrument line items spanning Liber 571 through Liber 1232, i.e., an early set (L571) through a much later set (L1232). Liber numbers increase roughly monotonically with recording date in most Michigan counties, so this ordering is a reasonable (not certain) proxy for chronology.

**[inference]** Reading the sequence as chapters:
1. **L571 series** (earliest): a DEQ Notice, two Warranty Deeds, two Quit Claims, and a Personal Representative's Deed (estate transfer) — looks like an early-2000s ownership-establishing cluster, consistent with the "2002" starting point in the work order.
2. **L1070–L1083 series**: two Quit Claims (L1070), then at L1083 a dense cluster — QC, Deed, **two Condo Master Deeds**, Restrictions, an **Option Agreement**, and the alley-vacation reference (L1083 P976) baked into the current legal description. This is almost certainly the creation of the "Petoskey Street Commons"/Petoskey Pointe condominium regime.
3. **L1090–L1163**: a 1st Amendment, more Restrictions, then a run of distress/litigation instruments — Notice of Lis Pendens, Court Order, Affidavit re: Additional Lien(s) (recorded twice per the county's own listing), Sheriff's Deed, another QC, Notice of Bankruptcy, a Covenant Deed, and a "CD".
4. **L1207 cluster**: Final Judgement, then a Warranty Deed for "condo units only," a QC for "land," and **two condo-termination instruments** — i.e., the condo regime being wound down.
5. **L1232**: a "CO" entry, immediately preceding/around the 11/07/2018 combination date on the parcel record — plausibly a Combination Order tying the block back into the single current parcel.

**[fact]** No dates, prices, or grantor/grantee names are available from BS&A for any of this — the Sale History grid was empty. Confirming actual dates and party names for each instrument requires the RoD index or a Liber/Page document pull. **Not yet done.**
