# T-7 · Venue comparator extension — bounding `S` (spillover) and `V_amph`

Strategy-side extension; `docs/AMPHITHEATRE_COMPARATORS.md` (design-side, read-only)
covers physical form. This file bounds the *economics*. Everything below is
[inference] from cited public reporting or [assumption] where scaled; no venue-level
audited financials were obtained (records/leads listed at end).

## Measured anchors (cited)

- **Levitt Pavilion Dayton** (free-concert model, downtown, ~5k lawn): 2024 —
  **72,450 attendance across 44 free concerts**; survey basis: ~1/3 of visitors spend
  ~$60 downtown before/after → **≈ $1.5M/yr downtown spend** (Dayton Daily News).
  Per-event spillover ≈ **$33k**.
- **Levitt Denver**: >110,000 season attendance; ">$10M in free concerts each year"
  (programming value, not budget) (levitt.org).
- **Levitt operating model**: majority community-funded (donors/sponsors/grants) —
  i.e., `V_amph` for a free-series venue is a *funded cost center with external
  spillover benefits*, not a profit center (levitt.org).
- **Frederik Meijer Gardens** (capacity-matched ~1,900; the design comparator): paid
  series ~30 shows/season, tickets commonly $45–90 — demonstrates a *paid* model at
  Petoskey's physical scale, but embedded in a nonprofit garden with membership base
  [inference].

## Brackets for the model (Petoskey bowl: 1,243 validated seats, seasonal)

- **Spillover `S`** [assumption, scaled from Dayton per-event ≈ $33k at ~1,650/event
  average draw]: Petoskey draw ceiling ≈ 1,243 formal (+ event floor). At 60–100% fill
  and the same 1/3 × $60 spend behavior (downtown is 1 block away — favorable):
  **$15k–25k downtown spend per event**; at 20–40 events/season:
  **`S` ≈ $0.3M–1.0M/yr** of downtown spending. Note this is sales-channel value —
  it does NOT appear in the T-6 property-tax arithmetic except via long-run taxable
  uplift.
- **`V_amph` operating** [assumption bracket]: free/community model (Levitt-like)
  ≈ **$0.5–1.5M/yr funded budget**; paid model (Meijer-like) can approach breakeven
  at Petoskey scale only with strong per-cap and sponsorship [inference — Meijer's
  garden context is not separable from its concert P&L on public data].
- **Joint read with T-6:** the amphitheater's fiscal case cannot rest on the
  29-parcel frontage uplift (breakeven 26.6% is implausible); it rests on `S`
  (sales/vitality channel), the wider-than-frame downtown premium, and avoided `E`
  — matching the model's structure. Evidence line only; no strategy call.
