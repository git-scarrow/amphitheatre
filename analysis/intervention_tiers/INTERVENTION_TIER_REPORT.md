## Is ambitious_shaped_bowl genuinely high-return?

Checked against the three places an accounting artifact could hide:

1. **Same evaluator, same rates.** All tiers run the identical fingerprinted evaluator and the identical cost ranges; the decomposition table above traces every seat to an operation.
2. **DEM-computed vs proxy seats.** +233 of the ambitious Band-A gain comes from row promotion + re-risering, costed from the DEM along bay centrelines (21 CY) — consistent with the fixed-stage sweep finding that capacity scales cheaply by adding terrain-supported rows. +149 seats come from the N1 east extension, which is a contour-walk UPPER BOUND shared by optimized and ambitious alike (it does not differentiate the tiers).
3. **No buried regressions.** Ambitious passes every hard check (min C 92 mm, fully formal: True), triggers no walls, and its max incremental depths (1.0/2.8 ft) stay inside the 3 ft caps.
4. **One known accounting effect, now stated.** The ladder marginal (+119 Band-A for ~$3,950) flatters ambitious because optimized absorbed the stage + apron structure costs. The order-independent rate is +382 QA for ~$56k vs baseline (≈6.9 QA/$1k) — still strong, not near-free.

**Verdict: ambitious_shaped_bowl is a genuinely high-return shaped-naturalistic upgrade within the model — “+233 DEM-backed seats for ~21 CY, plus +149 N1 upper-bound seats” — with one accounting effect disclosed (ladder ordering, point 4) and one validation caveat: everything beyond the baseline is analysis-tier pending re-emission and re-validation (Rules 3/5) and a Rule 9 stage decision.**
