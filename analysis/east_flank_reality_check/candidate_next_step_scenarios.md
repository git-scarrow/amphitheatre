## Scenario B — East-wrap seating extension

**Extend contour-bay seating farther onto the E/SE/Petoskey Street flank.**

**TERRAIN CONFIRMED (2026-06-06):** Direct seating-axis profile shows R130–190 ft has the same slope character (29–42%, avg ~30%) as the current seating zone. No escarpment. The terrain doesn't change character beyond row 16.

Build on `design_corner_bays.py` logic. Relax `DRDT_EAST` (currently 0.7) and extend the east-bay section farther toward Petoskey Street. Rows can reach R≈140–190 ft before the terrain flattens into the upper plateau at R~190–205 ft.

| Metric | Estimate |
|--------|---------|
| Formal seats | ~1,300–1,600 (additional 170–470 seats) |
| Additional rows | 3–8 additional contour bays above current R130 stop |
| Fan angle | ~150–170° |
| Inner/outer radius | ~85 / ~140–190 ft |
| Max distance from stage | ~140–190 ft |
| Earthwork | ~50–100 CY shallow fine-grading; no walls (terrain is the same character as existing rows) |
| ADA | East-sector seats accessible from Route B + Petoskey Street plateau approach |
| Bay view | East row ends have angled bay view (NNW looking past the stage) |
| Cost tier | **Budget to low-moderate** |
| Gating factor | Field survey for construction grading; property boundary confirmation |

**Key finding:** The terrain supports this extension. The question shifts from "can the terrain support more rows?" (yes) to "how many rows, what fan angle, and how to end cleanly at the upper plateau?"

## Scenario C — Continuous civic bowl (broadest wrap)

**Treat S, SE, and E walls as one continuous seating landform; explore 130°–170° fan.**

This is the full civic-bowl concept. Contour bays span from the south (Mitchell Street side) around to the east (Petoskey Street side) with continuous contour-aligned rows. The bend apex near the SE corner is the formal hinge.

| Metric | Estimate |
|--------|---------|
| Formal seats | ~1,400–1,800 (planning estimate) |
| Fan angle | 130° → 150° → 170° (three sub-options) |
| Inner/outer radius | ~85 / ~130–155 ft |
| Max distance from stage | ~130–155 ft |
| Earthwork | ~50–120 CY shallow fine-grading (more bays = more strip grading) |
| Walls | None assumed if east flank is continuous bowl wall; possible low seat walls at eastern row ends near Petoskey Street |
| ADA | Multiple dispersion points; Petoskey Street approach adds third accessible entry |
| Bay view | Mid-range; south-end seats face slightly eastward past stage — some partial bay view |
| Cost tier | **Moderate** |
| Gating factor | Full east-flank terrain survey; civil/geotech confirmation |

Sub-options:
- **C-130°:** Close to current geometry, adds 100–150 east seats
- **C-150°:** Significant east wing, adds 300–500 seats
- **C-170°:** Near full civic bowl; row ends approach Petoskey Street directly

## Scenario D — Petoskey Street overlook / eastern civic edge

**Use the upper eastern plateau as a public overlook, entry terrace, and promenade.**

Regardless of seating extent, the upper east plateau can be a civic element:
- Public walking terrace at the top of the east bowl wall
- Accessible arrival point from Petoskey Street sidewalk
- View overlook toward the stage and bay (looking west/NNW from the east rim)
- Connection between Lake Street and Mitchell Street along the east park edge

| Metric | Estimate |
|--------|---------|
| Additional seating | None (or 20–50 informal/casual overlook seats) |
| Terrace width | ~10–20 ft promenade at the top of the east bowl wall |
| Grade | Subject to terrain forensics; aim for accessible ≤5% longitudinal |
| Access | Petoskey Street sidewalk tie-in; ADA ramp from terrace down to seating or promenade |
| Earthwork | ~10–30 CY depending on existing grade at eastern plateau |
| Walls | Possible 1–2 ft terrace definition wall at the bowl rim lip |
| Bay view | From the eastern overlook, looking west across the bowl — foreground is the stage, background is the bay |
| Cost tier | **Budget to low-moderate** (mostly finish grading + paving) |
| Gating factor | Property boundary confirmation (B-1 in gating dossier); Petoskey Street ROW / sidewalk tie-in |

This scenario can be combined with any of A–C.

## Scenario E — Pavilion / treated upper east edge

**Light pavilion, shade structure, or service kiosk on the upper eastern plateau.**

The upper plateau could support:
- A small restroom and/or service building
- A shade canopy or performance support structure
- A concession / ticket point
- A small event-support shelter

| Metric | Estimate |
|--------|---------|
| Footprint | ~400–1,200 sq ft (single building or small pavilion) |
| Height | One story; design to avoid blocking stage sightlines from east-sector seats |
| Earthwork | Minimal — plateau grading + pad |
| Utilities | Water, sanitary, electrical (likely already on or near Petoskey Street) |
| Cost tier | **Moderate to major** (building + utilities) |
| Gating factor | Property boundary; City Parks program; B-1 survey; utility scan |

Sightline impact test needed: a building on the upper east plateau would appear behind east-sector seats. If the building is set back from the bowl rim, it may appear as a backdrop visible from west-sector (south/bay-side) seats. Low-profile / glass / permeable structure preferred.

## Scenario F — Low terrace / architectural wall scheme

**Allow modest retaining walls, seat walls, and terrace walls where they improve form.**

Cap initial study at walls ≤4 ft unless a specific larger intervention is justified. Compare directly to Scenario A (no-wall baseline) on cost and civic quality.

| Metric | Estimate |
|--------|---------|
| Wall heights | 1–4 ft at terrace edges, row ends, and Petoskey rim |
| Wall length | ~30–80 linear ft (planning estimate) |
| Wall type | Low masonry or concrete seat walls; low retaining walls at promenade edges |
| Additional seats | ~50–150 (better terrace geometry unlocks additional rows) |
| Earthwork | Slightly less grading needed per terrace level (walls substitute for some cut/fill) |
| Cost tier | **Moderate** (wall cost + standard earthwork) |
| Gating factor | Geotech for any wall > 2 ft; structural PE |

This is a legitimate design vocabulary. Epidaurus, Minack, outdoor venues worldwide use low stone terrace walls as amphitheatre character. Treating a 2-ft seat wall as a failure mode is architecturally incorrect.

## Scenario G — Net-zero earthwork civic bowl

**Permit cut/fill rebalancing across the pit; borrow from over-steep or excess zones.**

The pit is cut-dominant everywhere. A net-zero scheme permits:
- Cut from the bowl wall where it is steeper than needed (removing a proto-escarpment if confirmed)
- Fill used to improve: seating terraces, ADA landings, stage/orchestra pad, drainage features, Petoskey Street rim terrace
- Track cut and fill volumes separately by zone

| Zone | Cut potential | Fill destination |
|------|--------------|-----------------|
| Upper east rim (if escarpment confirmed) | Up to ~100 CY | Fill to widen upper plateau terrace |
| SE corner over-steep sections | Up to ~50 CY | Orchestra pad fill (already cut-balanced) |
| Upper S rim (rim-flatten zone) | ~30–60 CY | ADA landing fills / promenade |
| Drainage swales / overflow channel | ~50–100 CY | Compacted berm at rim |

| Metric | Estimate |
|--------|---------|
| Net earthwork | Near zero (by design) |
| Cut | ~300–500 CY (various zones) |
| Fill | ~300–500 CY (destinations above) |
| Additional seats enabled | ~100–300 (improved terrace geometry) |
| ADA improvement | Dedicated grading budget improves promenade pass rate (currently FAIL) |
| Cost tier | **Moderate** (more earthwork but no import/export) |
| Gating factor | Phase I/II ESA (contamination check for reuse); geotech for slope stability |
