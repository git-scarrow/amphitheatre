# Executive Summary — Petoskey Pit Amphitheater & Civic Event Garden

**Downtown Petoskey, Emmet County, Michigan** — an outdoor amphitheater and civic event garden
set into the "Petoskey Pit," a closed-surface depression (the 2007 Petoskey Pointe excavation)
in the downtown block at 200 E Lake St, across US-31 from Bayfront Park. The site is privately
owned (not Bayfront Park, not City-owned) and is not on Little Traverse Bay or the Bear River
mouth; "bay" throughout means the view the upper rows maximize toward Little Traverse Bay.

> from public LiDAR plus labelled assumptions. No soil, groundwater, contamination, or

## The concept in one paragraph

The bowl is turned into a venue from the **bottom up**: a stormwater treatment cell at the
floor, a raised event floor above the design flood pool, terraced seating climbing the
continuous **~20° S/SE/E bowl wall**, and a garden ring on the open bayward flank. The
audience sits on the **S/SE/E arc and faces northwest (azimuth ~330°) toward the bay and the
evening light**, looking out over the stage and a reflecting treatment pool to Little Traverse
Bay. The event floor elevation is set by **stormwater and groundwater logic, not by the
existing low point**, and the bowl bottom becomes a water-quality feature rather than occupied
space — exactly the design intent.

## What the analysis established (planning-grade)

- **The venue floor is set at 612.5 ft NAVD88** (recommended band 612.0–613.0). This sits ~1.2
  ft above the 100-yr design pool (611.31 ft, worst-soil case) and clears the 500-yr check
  (611.78 ft). The bowl bottom is **left at ~609.1 ft as a bioretention/treatment cell** — not
  excavated.
- **The bowl is hugely oversized as detention.** It holds **5.69 ac-ft** to its natural spill
  at 618.04 ft; even the 500-yr storm in the worst soil uses only ~17% of capacity. The design
  is **inflow-limited, not capacity-limited.**
- **Seating — Scenario E three-section civic bowl** (east / bend / south sections, **~130°
  effective span**), drawn and validated on the real emitted surface. Band-A validated capacity
  by adopted tier: **1,243 seats (baseline)**, **+114 → 1,357 (modest normalization)**, or
  **+262 → 1,505 (ambitious — the validated Pareto knee)** — all **geometric planning estimates,
  _not_ a code occupant load or operational event cap** (those require an egress/code analysis
  and a per-event permit; see `POST_EMISSION_DECISION_MEMO.md`). Sightlines meet the 90 mm
  C-value target on the emitted grade. The ~20° bowl wall means **engineered ADA access is
  required** — provided as **8.33% switchback ramps with landings, no retaining walls**.
- **Earthwork is modest and geometry-backed: 500.8 CY total** (restored formal treads +
  row-9 cross-aisle + two ADA switchback ramps + drainage swales), computed per component on the
  emitted geometry. Adopting the modest or ambitious seating tier adds only **25.3 or 47.3 CY**.
  **No structural retaining walls** are required: the earlier 6–14 ft wall figure was an artifact
  of the superseded narrow-fan, 30-row geometry — a design choice, **not a terrain law**
  (`analysis/east_flank_reality_check/REASSESSMENT_MEMO.md`). The 500.8 CY total **excludes** the
  stage refit, which is not yet adopted (see below).
- **A controlled outlet + armored emergency spillway** convert the closed bowl into a safe,
  metered-release basin: forebay → bioretention WQ cell (WQv 0.094 ac-ft) → control structure
  (orifice + weir) → outfall, plus a **612.0-ft emergency spillway** that engages below the
  occupied floor and clears the 500-yr surface storm with freeboard.
- **View and glare are separable.** The brief assumed the bay view and the low-evening-sun
  glare share the NW axis. The sun geometry shows otherwise: the **sun never sets on the ~330°
  view axis** (the most northerly sunset is az 305° at the summer solstice). Glare lives in a
  **W–WNW sector (≤308°), offset ~25–40° to the audience's left.** Recommendation: keep the
  **315°–345° bay-view corridor open** and put an **18–26 ft glare grove + an asymmetric WNW
  stage-backdrop wing in the 280°–308° sector** — which also shades the treatment cell.

## The stage is not yet resolved (Rule 9 OPEN)

The seating surface is validated, but the **stage geometry is not adopted**. The inherited
az-150° stage carries a +25.6° audience-axis mismatch and a −22.5 ft lateral offset against the
validated seating fan, and the tested typologies (P_opt, faceted aprons) remain **candidates,
not decisions**. Closing this **Rule 9** item is a live decision, independent of the
seating-tier choice; the 500.8 CY total above **excludes** any stage refit. See
`POST_EMISSION_DECISION_MEMO.md` (Decision 2).

## The receiving water drives the stormwater strategy

The bowl overflows toward the **Bear River mouth / Little Traverse Bay — a restored coldwater
fishery.** The treatment train is built for **infiltrate-first, shade, sediment-trap, and
energy-dissipate**, and assumes EGLE stormwater, Part 91 erosion/sediment, Part 303 wetland,
and NPDES/CGP review will all apply.

## The honest uncertainties (what could move the design)

Four field unknowns dominate, in priority order:

1. **The City storm-sewer network** — the only input that could change the answer. LiDAR
   cannot see piped flow; if the network routes a large area into the pit, the flood pool,
   floor, and spillway must be re-derived.
2. **Seasonal high groundwater** near the bowl floor — likely the elevation-setting input; a
   local perched table would force a permanent pool.
3. **Infiltration rate + ~20° bowl-wall slope stability** — set the treatment regime
   (underdrain?) and confirm the terraced wall plus Scenario E ramp cuts are stable under
   seating loading (a routine slope-stability check, **not** a retaining-wall premise).
4. **Boundary/shoreline extent + wetland delineation** — set what is buildable and where it
   can legally discharge.

All field, permitting, and approval prerequisites — each with an owner and purpose — are in

## Datum note

All elevations are **NAVD88 (Geoid12A), international feet** (the brief's "US survey feet" is
corrected — Michigan uses international feet). Bay level (~581 ft IGLD85) reconciles to
**~581.16 ft NAVD88** using the **+0.162 ft** offset **confirmed 2026-06-06 via NOAA VDatum**
(`gating_dossier.md` gate A-1; the prior assumed +0.40 ft is superseded — ~0.24 ft high, no
design decisions change).
