## Project description

The proposed project involves:
- Approximately 2 acres of earthwork within a closed bowl depression (floor ~609 ft NAVD88, rim ~618 ft);
- Grading of ADA access ramps (8.33% switchbacks with landings) into a continuous ~20° (≈35%) south/southeast/east bowl wall — maximum design cut/fill approximately 1.0/1.6 ft; no structural retaining walls are proposed;
- A stormwater bioretention/wet treatment cell at the bowl bottom (elevation ~609.1 ft NAVD88);
- A controlled outlet structure discharging toward Little Traverse Bay / Bear River;
- Approximately 2,200 CY net earthwork (cut + rim notch); surplus material targeted for on-site reuse as berms.

The bowl is the **2007 construction excavation** of the stalled Petoskey Pointe hotel/condo project (block demolished 2006, excavated 2007, no foundation built). The block previously carried an auto dealership (Petoskey Ford) and is an EGLE Part 201 site with UST history. Fill/debris placed in or around the excavation since 2007, remnant shoring/foundations, and the disturbed condition of the pit walls are **decision-critical** unknowns that coordinate with the environmental records review.

## Scope of services requested

### C-1 — Soil borings and field infiltration tests

- Minimum of **[4–6] soil borings** (exact count and placement to be proposed by the geotechnical engineer), targeting:
  - At least 2 borings at the bowl bottom / proposed treatment-cell location (~609 ft)
  - At least 2 borings within the proposed seating fill zones (mid-bowl, ~614–618 ft)
  - At least 1 boring at the proposed stage/forecourt fill zone (~613 ft)
  - At least 1 boring on the SE/SSE rim wall where cuts are deepest
- Borings to **refusal or 30 ft below existing grade**, whichever comes first.
- Hydrologic Soil Group (HSG) determination and USDA soil classification.
- **Field infiltration testing** (falling-head or double-ring infiltrometer) at the bowl bottom / treatment-cell elevation; report design infiltration rate for underdrain sizing.
- Deliverable: Boring logs, lab test results (grain size, Atterberg limits, density, infiltration), HSG classification, and recommendations for the stormwater treatment cell (infiltrate vs. underdrain vs. lined/permanent pool).

### C-2 — Seasonal high groundwater monitoring

- Install **minimum 2 observation wells** at the bowl bottom (~609 ft zone), screened across the likely water table horizon.
- Monitor water levels through **at least one complete spring wet season** (target: read spring 2027 high).
- Report: seasonal high groundwater elevation (NAVD88), separation from the proposed bowl bottom (609.1 ft) and event floor (612.5–613.0 ft), and recommendation on whether a liner or permanent pool is required.

> **Note:** Well installation should proceed as soon as possible (fall 2026 preferred) to capture the spring 2027 high-water reading. This is the single most likely input to reset the event-floor elevation.

### C-3 — Slope stability analysis, S/SE/E bowl wall

- Evaluate stability of the continuous ~20° (≈35%) south/southeast/east bowl wall under:
  - Existing conditions;
  - Proposed terraced-seating loading (stepped seat risers founded on the graded rake);
  - Proposed ADA switchback ramp cuts and landings (maximum cut ≈1.0 ft);
  - Seismic (as applicable per Michigan code).
- The design grades all access with 8.33% switchback ramps (max cut/fill ≈1.0/1.6 ft); no structural retaining walls are proposed. Low (1–4 ft) seat/terrace walls, if used, are civic design elements, not geotechnical retention.
- Address whether the 3:1 planning side-slope assumption for grade tie-ins is achievable in the site soils, or whether flatter slopes are required.
- Deliverable: Slope-stability analysis report with cross sections, factor of safety calculations, and side-slope recommendation.

### C-4 — Depth to bedrock

- Report depth to Petoskey limestone (or other bedrock) at each boring location.
- Flag any locations where bedrock is encountered within the proposed excavation envelope.
- Assess implications for excavation method and cost, and for infiltration feasibility (karst / solution porosity potential).

### C-5 — Excavation floor/wall condition and post-2007 fill

- Characterize the condition of the 2007 excavation: disturbed vs. native material at the floor and walls, any post-2007 fill or debris (depth, composition, consistency, origin), and any remnant shoring, footings, or utilities from the demolition/excavation era.
- Review available 2005-era project geotechnical reports (being requested from City/EGLE records) and reconcile with new borings.
- Coordinate findings with the environmental records review — borings may serve dual purpose for environmental screening samples (confirm with the ESA consultant).
- Deliverable: Written assessment of excavation condition, fill character, and implications for earthwork, settlement, and infiltration.
