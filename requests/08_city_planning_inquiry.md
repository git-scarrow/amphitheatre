**Re: Former Petoskey Pointe block (200 E Lake St) — planning questions from a resident study**

Dear Mr. Iacoangeli,

I am a resident conducting a self-funded, planning-grade study of the former Petoskey Pointe
excavation — the block bounded by Lake, Mitchell, and Petoskey Streets and US-31. The study
asks whether the existing bowl could support a small terraced civic amphitheater and event
garden, using the excavation's own geometry (the upper rows have an imperfect but real view
of Little Traverse Bay, and the concept is designed around maximizing it) and treating the
bottom as stormwater detention/treatment. So far this is desktop analysis only — LiDAR
terrain, hydrology, sightline and sun studies. I have not approached the property owner and
nothing has been proposed to the City; before going further I would like to understand the
block's planning status.

I would appreciate the Planning Department's help with a few questions:

1. **Site status.** Are there active site-plan approvals, development agreements, or pending
   applications for the block? Does the 2005-era Petoskey Pointe approval retain any legal
   effect?

2. **City plans.** How do the current master plan and downtown planning documents treat the
   block? Has any civic, public-space, or public-private concept for it been evaluated by
   the City over the years?

3. **Zoning.** The parcel shows as B-2 Central Business District. Would an outdoor assembly /
   civic amphitheater use be conceivable under B-2 (by right, special use, or PUD), or would
   it require rezoning?

4. **History the City holds.** Are there City-held studies of the excavation — geotechnical,
   drainage, or structural — from the 2005–2009 project era or since? (I am separately
   requesting storm-sewer records from Public Works, and EGLE's environmental file for the
   block.)

5. **Process.** If the study matures into something worth discussion, what is the right
   path — and is there existing City dialogue with the owner that a civic concept should be
   aware of?

I am happy to meet and to share the full technical package. Thank you for your time.
