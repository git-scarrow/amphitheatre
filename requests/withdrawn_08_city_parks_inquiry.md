**Re: Bayfront Park — planning questions about the west-end depression ("the pit")**

Dear Mr. Klingelsmith,

I am a resident conducting a self-funded, planning-grade feasibility study of the bowl-shaped
depression at the west end of Bayfront Park — specifically whether it could function as a
small terraced amphitheater and civic event garden, with the bowl bottom doing double duty as
stormwater treatment. The work so far is desktop analysis (LiDAR terrain, hydrology, sightline
and sun studies); nothing has been surveyed, permitted, or proposed to the City. Before taking
it any further I would like to understand the City's plans and constraints for that corner of
the park, and I would welcome the chance to share what the study has found.

I would appreciate the Department's help with the following questions:

1. **Master plan status.** How does the current Parks & Recreation Master Plan treat Bayfront
   Park's west end? Is an amendment cycle coming up, and has an amphitheater or performance
   use ever been evaluated for the park?

2. **Grant encumbrances.** Was Bayfront Park acquired or improved with Michigan Natural
   Resources Trust Fund, Land and Water Conservation Fund, or similar grant assistance? If
   so, which parcels are encumbered, and does the City hold the grant agreements? (This
   controls what conversions of use are possible and I want to respect it from the start.)

3. **The depression itself.** Does the City have institutional knowledge of the pit's origin —
   natural landform, former quarry, or filled ground? Any known drainage problems, standing
   water, or maintenance history there?

4. **Existing plans and studies.** Are there prior engineering, drainage, or improvement
   studies for the west end of the park that the Department could share or point me to?
   (I am separately requesting storm-sewer records from Public Works.)

5. **Process.** If the study matures into something worth public discussion, what is the right
   path — Parks & Recreation Commission first, or staff review before that?

I am happy to meet on site or at your office, and to share the full technical package. Thank
you for your time and for the Department's stewardship of the waterfront.
